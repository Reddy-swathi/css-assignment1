SPICES OF NALGONDA

Mirchi Bajji

Nalgonda Mirchi Bajji is a famous street food snack from Nalgonda, a town in Telangana, India. This spicy treat features large green chili peppers, which are slit open, stuffed with a tangy mixture (often made from tamarind, spices, and sometimes peanuts), dipped in chickpea flour batter, and deep-fried to crispy perfection.



It's typically served hot with a side of chopped onions and a squeeze of lemon juice, making it a flavorful and popular snack among locals and visitors alike.

Sarvapindi

Nalgonda Sarvapindi is a popular traditional snack from the Nalgonda district in Telangana, India. It's a savory pancake made from rice flour, chana dal (split chickpeas), peanuts, sesame seeds, green chilies, curry leaves, and spices.





The dough is flattened in a pan and cooked until crispy. It's loved for its crunchy texture and spicy flavor, making it a favorite tea-time snack.


Jonna Rotte

Nalgonda Jonna Rotte is a famous traditional Indian bread made from jowar (sorghum) flour, originating from the Nalgonda district in Telangana. It's known for being healthy and nutritious, often enjoyed with various curries or chutneys.




This bread is typically round and cooked on a hot griddle until it becomes slightly crispy on the outside while remaining soft on the inside. It's a staple food in many households and is appreciated for its earthy flavor and high fiber content.
